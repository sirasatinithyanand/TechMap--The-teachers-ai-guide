🗺️ THE IDEA: TeachMap
"Google gives you 2 million results. TeachMap gives you the right three — for your class, your suburb, your students, right now. And next time Sarah searches? It already knows what worked."

Instead of building another boring search tool, we're building a VISUAL MAP where teachers navigate resources like a territory — curriculum strands are regions, resources are landmarks that light up based on the teacher's classroom context.

🧠 HOW IT WORKS — 4 PILLARS:

1️⃣ Classroom Fingerprint (60 sec setup)
Teacher tells us once: year level, subject, school location, topic, class context (e.g. 30% ESL students). We remember it forever and filter everything through it using the UDL (Universal Design for Learning) framework — so we're applying real educational science, not just vibes.

2️⃣ The Visual Territory Map (THE WOW MOMENT)
A beautiful illustrated map where resources appear as landmarks. Brighter = better fit. Teacher can toggle demographics in real time and watch the map re-render. This is our live demo showstopper.

3️⃣ Trust Scorecard (Responsible AI proof — 10 points right here)
Every resource gets scored on:
- Source reliability (verified publisher vs random upload)
- Curriculum alignment % (e.g. NSW Stage 5 Science)
- Local relevance score
- ESL accessibility rating
- "Why this?" AI reasoning snippet
No black box. Ever. Judges will love this.

4️⃣ One-Click "Localise This" Companion Guide
Instead of editing locked PDFs (copyright nightmare), we generate a Teacher's Companion Guide alongside the resource:
❌ "Amazon Rainforest" → ✅ "Daintree Rainforest, FNQ"
❌ "Snow leopard" → ✅ "Koala corridor destruction, NSW"
Plus UDL framework adaptations for visual learners, ESL students etc.

5️⃣ Class Pulse — The Learning Loop (NEW 🔥)
After using a resource, teacher answers ONE question:
"Did this resource land well with your class?"
🟢 Worked great / 🟡 Partially / 🔴 Missed the mark

That single signal feeds back into TeachMap's engine — resources that consistently work surface faster, ones that miss get buried. Think of it like a CI/CD pipeline for teaching quality. The platform continuously deploys better recommendations the more it's used. It's never static.

🎯 OUR PITCH HOOK:
"Meet Sarah. Year 9 Science teacher, Parramatta. 28 students, 30% ESL, 45 minutes to plan Monday's lesson. She doesn't need more resources. She needs THIS resource. And next week? TeachMap already knows what worked for her class."

💻 TECH STACK (low-code friendly):
- Framer/Webflow for the visual map UI
- Claude API for localisation briefs + trust reasoning
- Mapbox for the illustrated territory map
- Bubble.io or Next.js as glue

WHY THIS WINS ALL 4 CRITERIA:
✅ Human-centred — built around a real teacher's real problem
✅ Responsible AI — transparent Trust Scorecard, no black box
✅ Feasible — Companion Guide sidesteps copyright completely
✅ Creative — map metaphor + CI/CD feedback loop = never been done in EdTech
